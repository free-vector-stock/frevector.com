Privacy Policy — frevector.com

Last updated: June 2026

frevector.com is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what data may be collected when you visit our site and how it is used.

1. Data We Collect

When you visit frevector.com, the following anonymous data may be collected automatically:

  • Cookies
  • Browser and device information
  • IP address for anonymous analytical purposes only
  • Page visit and interaction data
  • Analytical usage information

2. How We Use Your Data

The collected data may be used for improving site performance, enhancing user experience, detecting and fixing technical issues, ensuring site security, and supporting content development.

3. Personal Data

Personal data such as your name or email address is only processed when you voluntarily provide it. frevector.com does not sell user data to third parties or share it for commercial purposes.

4. Google AdSense and Third-Party Advertising

This website uses Google AdSense, a web advertising service provided by Google LLC. Google AdSense uses cookies, including the DoubleClick cookie, to serve ads based on your prior visits to this website or other websites on the internet.

Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads to you based on your visit to this site and/or other sites on the internet.

You may opt out of personalized advertising by visiting: Google Ads Settings. For more information on how Google uses data when you use its partners' sites or apps, please visit: policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites.

5. Cookie Policy

Cookies are used on this site to support site functions, remember user preferences, and measure performance. You may limit or disable cookies through your browser settings. When you first visit our site, you will be asked to accept the use of cookies.

6. Data Retention

Collected data is retained only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy or to comply with legal obligations.

7. Data Security

We take necessary technical and administrative measures to protect your data. However, no data transmission over the internet can be guaranteed to be 100% secure.

8. Your Rights

Under GDPR, you have the right to access, correct, delete, or object to the processing of your personal data. To exercise these rights, please contact us.
